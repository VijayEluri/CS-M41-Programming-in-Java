Remarks and corrections to Section 1.1

Avoid copy-and-paste!

  • Especially at the beginning (for the first year, say) avoid copy-and-paste when writing programs.
  • Programming must come (partially) from the sub-consciousness --- "let your fingers do the job".
  • The errors you make when typing a program are useful for you to find and understand.
  • Programming is (partially) like chess-playing, you need to see the potential movers clearly before your eyes --- but if you are insecure about the basic building blocks of programming, then you are strongly handicapped --- you can't see clearly your options.

Error handling

  • A good program should be able to handle any form of user input!
  • So whatever you do, leaving out inputs, giving wrong inputs, etc., never should the program abort, but it should always give you reasonable feedback on your specific wrong inputs.
  • Like with most introductory textbook, this aspect in handled poorly in "Introduction to Programming in Java".
  • In the additional programs I handle these aspects of error handling, including exception handling.

On the importance of functions

  • Functions (also "procedures") are the heart of programming.
  • Functions inside a class have the reasonable name member function (that is, they are special members) in C++.
  • This is somewhat hidden with the Java-terminology of "method" --- these are just (member) functions!

Some explanations for "public"

  • The opposite of "public" is "private".
  • These are the two main access specifiers.
  • They do not relate to some form of "security".
  • But they are about the two levels of organisation of a program:
    1. To the "outside" we just provide the "public interface", that is, what the user really interests.
    2. While to the "inside" we make accessible also all "private details", that is, how the "business really works", not just its "public image".
